# CopperJoint Product Architecture

CopperJoint's catalog is organized along **four merchandising axes** — when writing copy or designing a page, decide which axis you're presenting against.

## Axis 1: Product Lines (the brand-tier architecture)

### Everyday Line — `copperjoint.com/pages/everyday-comfort`
**Audience:** Professionals, travelers, nurses, teachers, anyone on their feet long hours.
**Positioning:** "Comfort without compromise."
**Tone:** Warm, "I've been there too."
**Hero products:** Copper Compression Socks, Long Compression Socks, Cushioned Ankle Socks 3-pack.

### Performance Line / Core210 — `copperjoint.com/collections/core210`
**Audience:** Athletes, runners, gym-goers, weekend warriors.
**Positioning:** "Train Stronger. Recover Smarter."
**Tone:** Disciplined, capable, in-motion.
**Key distinction:** **Core210 does NOT use copper.** It's the brand's compression-without-copper sub-brand, which gives them manufacturing flexibility and a way to compete on the performance axis without being tied to copper supply.
**Faith origin:** Named after Ephesians 2:10 ("we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works"). Referenced in About-page context; never preached in customer-facing product copy.
**Hero products:** Athletic Knee Sleeve, Athletic Cooling Long Compression Socks, Calf Sleeve Pro.

### Wellness Line — `copperjoint.com/pages/pain-pathways`
**Audience:** Older adults, post-surgical patients, arthritis sufferers, chronic pain.
**Positioning:** "Comfortable, medical-grade products for pain, swelling, and recovery."
**Tone:** Empathetic, clinically-credible, supportive.
**Hero products:** Lightweight Knee Brace, Hip Brace, Compression Gloves, CoreRelief Cream (topical extension).

### Specialty / Lifestyle Capsules
Limited-run capsule collections that broaden audience and refresh the catalog:
- **Pink Sock Collection** — artist collab with Paulina Jayne, "Joy in Motion" / "Keep Going" message bands. Women-focused.
- **Cushioned Ruffle Sock** — fashion crossover, "coquette" / "clean girl" trend tap.

## Bundle Strategy

12 bundles in the catalog. Bundles are priced to either (a) trigger the $60 free-shipping threshold or (b) deliver a ~15% bundle discount. **The Total Relief Bundle ($80.52, normally $99.05) is the hero bundle and the brand's strongest single product.**

Bundle audience archetypes:
- Starter ("All-Over Relief Starter," "Total Relief Bundle") — gift / new-customer
- Targeted condition ("Hand Support Relief," "Elbow Pain Relief," "Hip Pain Relief")
- Athletic ("Athletic Knee Support," "Runners Bundle," "Layered Athlete Support")
- Everyday ("Everyday Knee Support")

## Cross-Sell & Upsell Logic

When presenting any single product, the standard recommendation pattern is:

1. **Pair it** — show the matching opposite-side product (single sleeves should always upsell to pair-bundle)
2. **Layer it** — show the topical CoreRelief Cream as an add-on
3. **Care for it** — show the Delicate Laundry Bag accessory
4. **Replace it** — set the customer's expectation that compression should be replaced every 4–6 months (this is in the FAQ) — and **CopperJoint currently has no subscription product, which is a clear gap**

## Pricing Tiers (use these brackets when designing product cards)

- Accessories & singles: **$10–25** (single sleeves, gloves, laundry bag)
- Pairs & 3-packs: **$20–40** (sock 3-packs, sleeve pairs)
- Bundles: **$60–100** (sized to trigger free shipping)
- Topical / lifestyle extensions: **$15–25** (CoreRelief Cream)

## Voice Per Product Line (recap)

| Line | Voice cue | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Everyday | Warm, "I've been there" | Don't sound athletic-bro |
| Core210 | Disciplined, capable, technical | Don't get preachy about the faith origin |
| Wellness | Empathetic, clinically credible | Don't sound condescending to older buyers |
| Pink Sock / Ruffle | Playful, expressive | Don't read as childish or unserious |
