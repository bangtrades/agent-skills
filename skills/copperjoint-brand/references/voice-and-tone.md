# CopperJoint Voice & Tone

The CopperJoint voice is **clinical-but-warm**. It earns trust through hedged language and named expertise, not through hype. It treats the customer as an adult who is dealing with real discomfort, not a brand-affinity target.

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Clinically Hedged
- We say what compression is *shown to* do, not what it *will* do.
- We reference Dr. Strasser by credential, not by anecdote.
- We use words like "may support," "helps reduce," "is associated with," "many people experience."
- We never use "cures," "guarantees relief," "100% pain-free," "miracle," "breakthrough."

### 2. Warm & Conversational
- We write like a thoughtful nurse explaining something to a patient, not like a sales page.
- We use everyday language: "long shifts," "all-day comfort," "on your feet," "after a workout."
- We ask plain questions: "Does wearing support make joints weaker?" "How long should I wear it?"

### 3. Owner-Operator Honest
- We are family-run and we say so without false modesty.
- We acknowledge what compression *cannot* do as clearly as what it *can*. "Compression is a support tool, not a substitute for medical diagnosis."
- We tell customers when to escalate to a professional.

### 4. Quietly Confident
- We don't shout. We don't use exclamation points in product copy.
- The product does the work; the copy describes it cleanly.
- The hero stat ("99% potency," "15-20 mmHg," "15k+ products donated") is presented numerically, not breathlessly.

## Voice Don'ts (these will read as off-brand)

- ❌ "Say goodbye to pain forever"
- ❌ "The #1 best compression sleeve in the world"
- ❌ "Doctors hate this one weird trick"
- ❌ "Limited time! Buy now!" (urgency manipulation)
- ❌ "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "disrupting" (tech-bro vocabulary)
- ❌ "Just for athletes" or "for the elite" (we're for everyone)
- ❌ All-caps headlines longer than 3 words
- ❌ Multiple exclamation points
- ❌ Emojis in product copy (sparingly OK in social only)
- ❌ Religious overtone in customer-facing copy (Core210 namesake is mentioned in About; never proselytized)

## Voice Do's

- ✅ "Built with orthopedic guidance"
- ✅ "Designed for everyday life"
- ✅ "Helps support comfort, movement, and recovery"
- ✅ "Trusted by nurses, teachers, travelers, and active adults"
- ✅ "Backed by our 90-day fit guarantee"
- ✅ "Reviewed by Dr. Nicholas Strasser, MD — Board-Certified Orthopedic Surgeon"
- ✅ Plain numbers: "2,200+ reviews," "15-20 mmHg compression," "since 2014"

## Tone Per Product Line

Voice is constant. Tone shifts.

### Everyday Line — warm, comfortable, "I get it"

> "Built for nurses on 12-hour shifts, teachers on their feet, and travelers on long flights. Compression that fits under your scrubs, your slacks, or your uniform — and feels good doing it."

### Performance Line / Core210 — capable, disciplined, in-motion

> "Train stronger. Recover smarter. Core210 is built for the athletes who treat their body like equipment they intend to keep using. Tested for high-impact, all-day wear."

### Wellness Line — empathetic, medical-credible, supportive

> "If you live with joint pain, swelling, or post-surgical recovery, comfort matters. The Wellness Line is built with clinical input to support what your body is already working on — without getting in the way of your day."

## Tone Per Channel

### Website (product pages)
Clean, factual, benefit-led. Lead with the use-case. Hedged claims. Numbers in headlines (mmHg, sizes, days).

### Email
First-person, conversational, "we" not "the company." Sign as "The CopperJoint Team" or sub-line items with Dr. Strasser if it's an education email.

### Social (Instagram / Facebook)
Friendlier, more personal. Customer stories. Behind-the-scenes from Nashville. Faith-adjacent quotes are OK on captioned text-art posts. Emojis sparingly — heart, sparkle, prayer hands are on-brand; flame, money-bag, party-popper are off-brand.

### Press releases
More formal but still warm. Lead with the human story ("family-run business launches…"). Always include the founder/family quote. Never lead with the SKU.

### Amazon listings
**Currently broken — uses legacy voice. Rewrite priority.** Should match the website voice, not the 2017 founder-era voice. Lead with the use-case, then the technical spec, then the support story (Dr. Strasser, 90-day fit guarantee, USA shipping).

### Dr. Strasser content (blog posts, email education, video transcripts)
First-person from Strasser. More technical vocabulary is acceptable here. Cite anatomy and mechanism plainly. Always end with "if your pain is acute, persistent, or accompanied by swelling that doesn't resolve, please consult your orthopedic physician."

- Revolutionary / breakthrough / game-changing

## Voice Self-Check (run before publishing anything)

1. Would Dr. Strasser put his name on this? (If not, rewrite.)
2. Have I made any unhedged medical claim? (If yes, hedge it.)
3. Have I used hype words ("revolutionary," "miracle")? (If yes, cut them.)
4. Is the customer treated as an intelligent adult? (If not, rewrite.)
5. Is the warmth there, or does it read sterile? (If sterile, add a human moment.)
6. Does the copy match the product line's tone? (Everyday warm / Performance disciplined / Wellness empathetic)
