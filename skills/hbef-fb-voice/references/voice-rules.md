# HBEF Facebook Voice — Detailed Rules

## Sentence rhythm

The default HBEF post structure is **3–6 short paragraphs**, each **1–3 sentences**. Sentences range **8–25 words**. Long, comma-heavy sentences appear only in formal recap posts and even then are broken up by mid-clause em-dashes or list structures.

**Cadence example (Skechers Walk recap):**

> A heartfelt THANK YOU to everyone who registered, cheered, and supported the 16th Annual Skechers Friendship Walk. [22 words]
>
> Thanks to your incredible efforts, we raised a record-breaking $269,812 for Hermosa schools! [13 words]
>
> This partnership with the Friendship Foundation and Skechers has now raised over $2.13M over 16 years, helping HBEF to fund vital educational programs that would otherwise be unavailable to our Hermosa students. [32 words — the longest sentence, used to deliver the impact context]
>
> We are deeply grateful to our school administration, teachers, students, families, and everyone who donated or shared their team links. [20 words]
>
> Your support was essential. [4 words — punch sentence]

Note the rhythm: long-short-long-medium-short. The four-word punch sentence ("Your support was essential.") is signature.

## Capitalization

- **Body copy:** sentence case. Always.
- **CTAs:** UPPERCASE button-style. `BUY TICKETS` / `DONATE NOW` / `REGISTER NOW` / `LEARN MORE` / `SUPPORT HBEF NOW` / `JOIN WAITLIST` / `JOIN THE AUCTION COMMITTEE`.
- **Headlines/section breaks in long-form posts:** Title Case with key words capitalized. `Annual Giving Campaign Raised A Record-Breaking $508,332`.
- **HBEF acronym:** always all caps. Never "hbef" lower-case.
- **Schools:** capitalize as proper nouns. `View`, `Vista`, `Valley`. Not "view school."
- **Tier names:** Title Case. `Brighter Benefactor`, `Diamond Level`. Not `diamond level`.
- **Event names:** Title Case. `Hearts of Hermosa`. `Skechers Friendship Walk`. `Moms' Night Out`. `Annual Giving Campaign`.
- **For dramatic effect** (rare): a single ALL-CAPS word mid-sentence is allowed, e.g. *"our guests BRING IT!"* or *"a heartfelt THANK YOU"*. Never two ALL-CAPS words in the same sentence.

## Punctuation

- **Exclamation marks:** Used liberally for excitement beats, but never stacked (`!!` ❌). One `!` per sentence max.
- **Em-dashes (`—`):** HBEF uses these freely for parenthetical context. *"This event sells out every year in record time—don't miss your chance to secure your spot!"*
- **Ellipses (`...`):** Sparingly, only for soft cliffhangers. *"Mark your calendar…"*
- **Hashtag mention:** never inline with body copy. Reserve for end-of-post stack.
- **Ampersand (`&`):** Used inside event titles and tier lists (`STEM, Music, & PE`). Don't replace `and` in running sentences.
- **Smart quotes:** Yes — `'` and `"`, not straight quotes.
- **Hyphen vs en-dash vs em-dash:**
  - Hyphen: compound words (`record-breaking`)
  - En-dash: date and time ranges (`2024–25 school year`, `6–11pm`)
  - Em-dash: parenthetical thought (`Hermosa schools—and the kids who depend on them—need our support`)

## Numbers

- **Dollars:** Always with `$` prefix. Use full numbers with comma separators when the amount is the headline: `$269,812`, `$1,265,000`, `$508,332`. Use abbreviations (`$1.5M`, `$2.13M`) when summarizing over time or when the precise number is unavailable.
- **Percentages:** Spelled with `%`, never "percent" in body. `5%`, `100%`.
- **Times:** Lowercase am/pm, no space, no period. `10am`, `7-10pm`, `6-11pm`. *Exception:* on tickets-on-sale alarms, the time is often uppercased for emphasis: `10AM!`.
- **Years:** `2024-25 school year` or `FY 2024-25` for fiscal year.
- **Dates:** Informal: `Saturday, May 9th, 6-11pm`. Formal: `May 9, 2026`.
- **Ordinals on event editions:** `16th Annual Skechers Friendship Walk`, `30th annual "Hearts of Hermosa"`. Cite the count when it's an anniversary moment.

## Pronouns and voice

- **First person plural — always.** `We`, `our`, `our community`, `our schools`, `our kids`, `our Hermosa community`. Never "the foundation," "the organization," "HBEF works to" (third person).
- **Direct address — "you":** `You make this possible`, `Your support was essential`, `Your generosity demonstrates…`. Use the parent reader as a collaborator, not a target.
- **The community as collective subject:** `Hermosa families`, `our Hermosa students`, `HVS community`, `HBCSD community`, `the entire HVS community`. Geographic + civic phrasing over generic "community."

## Acronym handling

- **Spell out on first use, abbreviate thereafter.** *"The Hermosa Beach Education Foundation (HBEF) celebrated…"* → "HBEF" after.
- **District abbreviations:** `HBCSD` (district), `HVPTO` (sister org). Spell out the first time in any post that mentions both.
- **School names short-handed:** *View*, *Vista*, *Valley* — locals know which is which. Don't expand to "Hermosa Valley School" mid-sentence unless required for an outside audience.

## Tone modifiers

| Mood | Phrases |
|---|---|
| Excitement | "amazing," "incredible," "epic," "fantastic," "unforgettable," "biggest…ever" |
| Gratitude | "heartfelt," "deeply grateful," "so grateful," "we are so grateful to…" |
| Inclusion | "every," "all," "the entire," "our community," "100%," "every family" |
| Specificity | "exactly," exact numbers, named people, named restaurants |
| Soft urgency | "act fast!" "don't miss your chance," "sells out quickly," "tickets are limited" |
| Civic mission | "bridge the gap," "fund the programs the state won't," "education truly matters" |

Avoid: "blessed," "humbled," "honored to" — too churchy / too award-show. HBEF is parent-civic, not awards-ceremony.

## Linking conventions

- **Always link partner names** when mentioned in event copy. The Bottle Inn, Palmilla, Martha's, Hook & Plow — each gets linked.
- **Always link sponsor names** in recognition posts. Sponsor logos get clickable href to the sponsor's site.
- **Always link the event page** when teasing/recapping an event.
- **Donate link:** Use `hbef.org/donate-now/` for direct giving, or `givebutter.com/[event-handle]` for event-specific Givebutter links.
- **Photos:** Credit `Jennifer Faulk Photography` (linked or unlinked) for event photos. Lana Lee Photography for Santa Photos.
- **Pinterest inspiration board:** When applicable, link to `pinterest.com/hbef90254/[board-name]` for attire inspiration before themed events.

## Emoji policy (the long version)

The cap is **1–2 emoji per post**, max **3 in event-night excitement posts**. From the HBEF palette:

### Core education / mission emoji
- 📚 — education, curriculum, counseling, program announcements. The mission-default.

### Event-night excitement
- 🎉 — celebration, record-breaking results
- 🪩 — disco / dance / party / DJ
- ❤️ — Hearts of Hermosa, community love
- 🎶 — music, band, DJ lineup

### Ticket / urgency
- 🚨 — tickets-on-sale alarm (paired with `SET YOUR ALARMS`)
- 🎟️ — tickets, raffle
- ⏰ — deadline reminder (rare)

### Themed accents
- ⚓ — "Come Sail Away" nautical themes
- 🤠 — "Boots on the Beach" western themes
- ☀️ — summer / outdoor / beach
- 🛟 — life-saver / "lifeguard" theme variants

### Forbidden / off-brand
- 🙏 — too churchy
- 💯 — HBEF says "100%" in words
- 👏 — clap-as-punctuation reads cringey
- 🔥 — off-brand (this isn't a hype account)
- 🥰 / 😍 — too gushing
- 💸 / 💰 — never (commodifies the mission)
- ✨ / ⭐ — only for very celebratory moments, sparingly

## Hashtag policy

Used **sparingly** — HBEF isn't a hashtag-heavy account. When used, always at the end on a dedicated last line, never inline.

Standard set:
- `#HBEF` / `#HBEF90254`
- `#HermosaBeach` / `#HermosaSchools`
- Event-specific: `#HeartsOfHermosa`, `#SkechersFriendshipWalk`, `#MomsNightOut`
- Mission: `#FundingMatters`
- `#SouthBay` for broader regional pull

**Cap:** 3–5 hashtags per post, all at the end. Skip hashtags entirely on intimate / behind-the-scenes / thank-you posts.

## Hyperlink behavior on Facebook

- Facebook will auto-card-preview the **first** URL it sees.
- For a post with one main CTA, put the link near the end so the preview pulls the right page.
- If you reference 2+ links (event page + sponsor site), think about which one deserves the card; structure accordingly.
- For Stories: short links only, native FB tags + sticker CTAs preferred over long URLs.

## Tax language placement

Required on any post that explicitly asks for a donation. Standard tail:

> *Tax-deductible! HBEF is a 501(c)(3) — EIN 33-0522270.*

Not required on event-recap, save-the-date, sponsor-spotlight, or program-update posts (where the ask is implicit, not explicit).

## What HBEF never says on Facebook

- "Please please please" or stacked pleading
- "You'll regret it if you don't…" — never guilt-trip
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "synergy," "leverage," "actionable" — corporate jargon
- "Beverly Hills schools have it, why don't we?" — HBEF doesn't frame as inferior, they frame as structural gap
- "Thoughts and prayers" or any religious framing
- "We're just a small organization" — false humility / undercuts impact
- "Click here" without a contextual CTA — always specific verbs (`BUY TICKETS`, `REGISTER NOW`)
