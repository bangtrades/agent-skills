# HBEF Facebook Voice — Skill

This skill captures the voice, structure, and content patterns HBEF uses on Facebook (facebook.com/HBEF90254) and adjacent short-form social surfaces (Instagram @hbef90254, Givebutter event descriptions, the Surf Report newsletter, event pages on hbef.org). It is the **voice layer** companion to **`hbef-brand`** (the visual layer). When a post ships with a graphic or photo card, read both skills.

## When to read what

- **`references/archetypes.md`** — Post archetype playbook. Read first when you know what kind of post you're writing (teaser, tickets-on-sale, recap, etc.).
- **`references/voice-rules.md`** — Sentence rhythm, capitalization, emoji policy, hyperlink etiquette, hashtag conventions.
- **`references/formula-library.md`** — Fill-in-the-blank formulas for the most common post types. Use when you need to crank out a post fast.
- **`references/theme-extension.md`** — How HBEF extends a campaign theme ("Come Sail Away", "Boots on the Beach") through every line of copy. Read for any themed event post.
- **`references/examples-corpus.md`** — Real HBEF post excerpts with source URLs. Use as ground truth when you're unsure if a phrasing feels HBEF.

Read this SKILL.md first, then the archetype + voice references, then write. **Don't invent phrasings from memory if you're unsure** — the corpus has the actual words.

## Voice DNA (one paragraph)

HBEF's Facebook voice is **earnest, specific, enthusiastic without being breathless**. It speaks as **"we" — a group of Hermosa parents talking to other Hermosa parents** about a shared project: keeping our schools great. It names names (Co-Chairs, donors, restaurant partners). It quotes real numbers (`$269,812`, never "over $250K"). It commits hard to a campaign theme and lets the metaphor run through every line ("Lido Deck," "Anchors Down," "Boots on the Beach"). Emoji are used — but **sparingly and signalingly** (📚 for an education update, 🚨 for an alarm-the-troops moment, 🪩 for a dance event), never as decoration. Every post ends with a clear next action: a date, a ticket link, a thank-you. **If a draft sounds like it could have come from a corporate communications team, it's wrong.** It should sound like Belinda just sat down at her kitchen table and typed it.

## Core voice rules (the short version)

1. **First person plural** — "we," "our community," "our schools," "our kids." Never "the foundation," "HBEF (third person)."
2. **Name the names** — Co-Chairs, donor lists, partner restaurants, school principals. Specificity is gratitude.
3. **Use real numbers** — exact dollar amounts (`$269,812`), exact dates (`Saturday, May 9, 6–11pm`), exact times (`10:00am`). Never "over $250K" when the post-recap is the moment to quote the precise figure.
4. **Theme-extend hard** — once an event theme is set, every CTA, every section header, every "save the date" extends the metaphor. (See `references/theme-extension.md`.)
5. **One emoji per beat, max** — restrained palette of ~6 emoji (see voice-rules.md). Decoration emoji (sparkles, stars) only in event-day excitement posts.
6. **All CTAs UPPERCASE or Title Case button-style** — `BUY TICKETS`, `DONATE NOW`, `REGISTER NOW`, `LEARN MORE`. Never just "click here."
7. **Soft urgency** — "Tickets sell out quickly, so act fast!" Not "ACT NOW OR YOU'LL MISS OUT FOREVER."
8. **Always close with a next action** — date, link, thank-you, or save-the-date for the next one. Posts don't trail off.
9. **Hyperlink generously** — every partner gets linked, every event page gets linked, every donate button gets linked.
10. **HBEF vs HVPTO clarity** — never let a parent confuse who funds what.

## The post archetype library

HBEF cycles through ~10 recurring archetypes. Knowing which one you're writing dictates the structure. Detailed templates live in `references/archetypes.md`; the headlines:

| # | Archetype | When | Signature beats |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Save-the-Date / Event Teaser** | 4–8 weeks before event | Theme name, date, location, 1-line excitement, ticket-info, image |
| 2 | **Tickets On Sale** | The morning tickets drop | 🚨 alarm, exact time, ticket tiers, first-day incentive, link |
| 3 | **Event Recap / Results** | 1–3 days after event | Dollar figure or "Well done, Hermosa!", thank-you list, save-the-date for next |
| 4 | **Check Delivery / Contribution Announcement** | Fall, after AG totals reconcile | Dollar contributed, list of programs funded, "without this funding…", gratitude |
| 5 | **Annual Giving Push** | August–September AG window | Progress to goal, $1K-per-child anchor, 100% participation framing |
| 6 | **Sponsor Spotlight** | When a Community Partner renews/upgrades | Tier acknowledgment, link to sponsor, 1-line thanks |
| 7 | **Volunteer Ask** | Any time roles need filling | Specific role, contact email, "inquiring doesn't commit you" |
| 8 | **Program/Impact Update** | When something tangible lands (counselor hired, lab built) | Concrete student impact, school name, year, hopeful close |
| 9 | **Press / External Coverage Share** | When local press features HBEF | Brief setup, link to article, photo credit |
| 10 | **Sign-up Reminder / Last Call** | 1–3 days before deadline | Soft urgency, specific deadline, sentence-2 link |
| 11 | **Behind-the-Scenes / Crew Shoutout** | Mid-campaign | Name the chairs/volunteers, photo, mission-tie close |
| 12 | **Photo Gallery Drop** | Days after the event when JFP photos are ready | Few words, photographer credit, link to gallery |

## Theme extension is HBEF's signature move

The single most identifiable feature of HBEF copy is how a campaign theme **propagates through every line**. When the 2026 Hearts of Hermosa theme was "Come Sail Away," every CTA, every section header, every label rewrote itself in nautical-yacht-rock-band voice:

- *Bar* → *Galley* ("Last Call at the Galley")
- *Event ends* → *Anchors Down*
- *Welcome* → *All Aboard*
- *Food area* → *Lido Deck*
- *Donors* → *crew* / *captains at the helm*
- *Setting up* → *provisioning for our biggest voyage yet*
- *Hello, friends!* → *Ahoy, friends!*

When the 2025 theme was "Boots on the Beach," the awards became "Saddled Up in Style," "Rodeo Royalty Couple," "Fashion Outlaw." When MNO 2026 is "Ocean Air & Epic Beats," the doors-open line becomes "Doors Open: 7:00pm 🪩 Epic Beats: 7:30pm 🎟️ Raffle Drawing: 8:30pm."

**Rule: if you're writing about a themed event, every metaphor-eligible word must align with the theme.** See `references/theme-extension.md` for the full "before → after" lexicon by theme.

## Emoji palette (HBEF's actual list)

Used in observed posts; do not stray:

| Emoji | Use | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| 📚 | Education / program / counseling / curriculum announcements | Most common in program-update posts |
| 🚨 | Urgent ticket-drop or sale alarm | Tickets-on-sale posts |
| 🪩 | Dance / party / MNO / DJ moment | Event-night posts |
| 🎟️ | Raffle, tickets, drawing | Always paired with ticket info |
| ❤️ | "Hearts of Hermosa" / community love beat | Hearts-related posts |
| ☀️ | Summer / beach / outdoor event | Occasional, mostly summer-coded |
| 🎉 | Celebration moment — record-breaking result | Recap and big-number posts |
| ⚓ | "Come Sail Away" theme accent | Theme-specific 2026 |
| 🤠 | "Boots on the Beach" theme accent | Theme-specific 2025 |
| 🎶 | Music callouts (band, DJ) | Entertainment lineup posts |

**Never use:** clapping hands as punctuation (👏), the corporate "100" 💯 (HBEF means "100%" in words), random fruits/animals, the prayer-hands 🙏 (too churchy), 🥰 / 😍 (too gushing), fire 🔥 (off-brand). HBEF leans **community-civic**, not influencer.

**Cap:** one emoji per "beat" of a post, two max in event-night excitement posts. Never emoji-bomb.

## Hashtag conventions

HBEF uses hashtags **sparingly** — they're not a hashtag-heavy account. Standard set when used:

- `#HBEF` — always available, identity tag
- `#HBEF90254` — full handle, often instead of plain HBEF
- `#HermosaBeach` — geo tag
- `#HermosaSchools` — district tag
- `#HeartsOfHermosa` — event-specific
- `#SkechersFriendshipWalk` — event-specific
- `#MomsNightOut` — event-specific
- `#FundingMatters` — mission tag (riffs on the primary tagline)
- `#100Percent` — participation campaign-coded
- `#SouthBay` — broader geo when appealing to area sponsors

**Cap:** 3–5 hashtags max per post, ALL at the end on a separate line or single closing line. Never inline. Never #HashtagsCamelCase like #ItTakesAVillage in body copy — feels forced.

## Sentence rhythm

The typical HBEF FB post is **3–6 short paragraphs**, each **1–3 sentences long**. The rhythm:

1. **Hook (1 sentence)** — theme reference, dollar figure, exclamation, or save-the-date callout
2. **Context (1–3 sentences)** — what happened, who showed up, what was raised, what's coming
3. **Gratitude or specifics (1–3 sentences)** — names of people/partners, specific programs funded, specific photos credited
4. **CTA (1 sentence)** — link to event page, donate URL, RSVP form, save-the-date

Variations exist (the long-form FAQ post for Flash Parties is one), but the 4-beat shape is the default. **Don't write a 12-paragraph essay for Facebook.**

## Things HBEF never does

- **Begging language** — "Please please please donate!!!" Never. Confidence + math, not pleading.
- **Guilt-tripping** — "Your kid won't have music class if you don't give!" Never. Frame as collective opportunity, not individual blame.
- **Corporate jargon** — "leverage," "synergy," "stakeholder engagement," "impactful." Never.
- **Vague abstractions** — "supporting our amazing community!" Be specific: "funding 3 full-time counselors and Spanish for 3rd–5th grade."
- **Naked pleas without a thank-you** — every ask includes a beat of gratitude for the existing community.
- **Stacking exclamation marks** — one `!` per sentence max, and most sentences should end in a `.`
- **All-caps for emphasis in body** — UPPERCASE is reserved for CTAs and headers. Mid-sentence shouting is off-brand.
- **Confusing HBEF with HVPTO** — always distinguish if both could apply.

## Working with the visual layer

If the post ships with a graphic (most do), pair this skill with **`hbef-brand`** to produce the image:

- **Image specs:** Facebook 1080×1080 square or 1080×1350 portrait. Instagram same. Use the brand palette from `hbef-brand/assets/brand-tokens.json`.
- **Typography on graphics:** Montserrat 200 light for hero numbers, Montserrat 700 bold uppercase for event names, BauhausBold for event title moments.
- **Photography:** Credit Jennifer Faulk Photography on event-recap photo cards.

When generating both a post and its graphic in one go, read `hbef-brand` → build graphic, then read this SKILL.md + appropriate archetype → draft the caption.

## Quick recipe — "I need to write an HBEF post about X"

1. **Identify the archetype** (see table above). If unsure, default to "Program/Impact Update" or "Event Recap" — those cover most cases.
2. **Identify the theme** (if it's a themed event). Pull the lexicon from `references/theme-extension.md` if available, or invent one and commit hard.
3. **Read the matching archetype template** in `references/archetypes.md`.
4. **Pull the formula** from `references/formula-library.md` if you want fill-in-the-blank speed.
5. **Draft with the 4-beat rhythm**: hook → context → gratitude/specifics → CTA.
6. **Apply emoji policy** — 1–2 max, from the palette.
7. **Hashtags** at the end, 3–5 max.
8. **Verify against the never-do list** above.
9. **If a graphic ships with it**, pair with `hbef-brand`.

## Final guardrails

- **Read the corpus before improvising.** `references/examples-corpus.md` has real HBEF posts with sourced URLs. The voice is in those words — don't drift.
- **Lengths:** Facebook caption ≤ 2,200 chars (FB cuts at ~480 chars before "See more"); Instagram caption ≤ 2,200 chars; Stories ≤ ~250 chars. Most HBEF FB posts run **150–600 chars in the visible portion**.
- **Tax language** is *not* required on every FB post (unlike formal documents), but **must appear** on any post that explicitly asks for a donation — typically as a brief "Tax-deductible! HBEF EIN 33-0522270" closer, often inside a parenthetical.
- **Hyperlinks on Facebook** auto-card-preview — usually one main link per post. If the post has 3+ links, the preview goes to the first; structure the post so the most important link is first.
- **When in doubt**, ask: would Belinda send this from her phone at her kitchen table? If yes, ship it. If it reads like a press release, rewrite.
