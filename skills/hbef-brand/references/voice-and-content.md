# HBEF — Brand Voice & Content Reference

Editorial voice rules, signature phrases, and content templates for HBEF deliverables. Pair with `SKILL.md` for visual styling and the relevant format reference for file mechanics.

## Voice in one paragraph

HBEF is **parent-led**, **neighborhood-scaled**, and **earnest**. The voice should read like a smart, organized parent talking to other parents at a school pickup — warm, specific, slightly aspirational about what Hermosa kids deserve, and unembarrassed about asking for money because the math is just true. Never corporate. Never desperate. Never preachy. The brand has a polite confidence: "Hermosa is structurally underfunded, and *this* is how we close the gap."

## Audience map

| Audience | Voice register | What to lead with |
|---|---|---|
| **HBCSD families** (parents) | Warm, parent-to-parent | Participation framing + suggested $1K-per-child anchor + concrete program list |
| **Local business sponsors** | Civic, ROI-aware | Tier benefits + brand visibility + community standing |
| **Existing donors / Diamond+** | Insider, low-pressure | Impact report + what their gift specifically funded + endowment posture |
| **HBEF board** (internal) | Operational, direct | Status, decisions needed, risks, asks — minute-style |
| **Investment Committee** | Peer portfolio-manager | Benchmarks, allocations vs target, attribution, peer comparison |
| **District administration / HBCSD board** | Partnership, deferential | Funded programs, allocation transparency, no overreach into governance |

## Signature phrases (use sparingly — these are anchors, not crutches)

- *"Funding matters. Your donation, their future."* — primary tagline
- *"100% Volunteers | 100% Parents."* — secondary tagline / position statement
- *"It Takes a Village OF 100%."* — campaign rallying cry for Annual Giving participation
- *"Funding education. Empowering students. Strengthening community."* — three-beat mission cadence
- *"Every dollar makes a real, tangible difference."* — small-donor inclusion
- *"$1,000 per enrolled child"* — suggested family gift anchor (always frame as suggested, never required)
- *"Bridging the gap of inadequate government funding."* — mission shorthand
- *"~5% of HBCSD's operating budget"* — quantified scale statement

## What to avoid

- Consultancy speak: *leverage, synergy, actionable insights, alignment, stakeholder*
- Hype: *game-changing, revolutionary, unprecedented, transformative*
- Defensive framing: *we're not just a fundraiser…* (don't apologize)
- Beverly Hills / wealth signaling: HBEF's identity is *not* a Basic Aid district — never imply Hermosa is rich. The structural funding-gap story is the whole differentiator.
- Saccharine: *precious little learners, miracle of education* — earnest, not gushing
- Acronyms without expansion on first use: spell out HBCSD, HVPTO, LCFF, IC the first time
- Conflating HBEF with HVPTO

## The funding-gap story (the core narrative)

When explaining *why* HBEF exists, work through this sequence — but compress to fit. Donor-facing materials need the headline beats; IC materials don't need it at all; board materials need the second-paragraph nuance.

> Hermosa Beach has excellent schools and a strong community — but the math doesn't add up. California's funding formula (LCFF) directs the most state dollars to districts with high concentrations of high-needs students, and the rest comes from local property taxes. HBCSD is a **Revenue Limit district**, meaning the state caps how much local property tax our schools can keep — and **less than 20%** of Hermosa Beach's property taxes actually flow to HBCSD. Pair that with Prop 13's hold on assessed values, and our schools end up among the **lowest-funded in California**. The gap between what HBCSD receives and what it costs to deliver a Hermosa-quality education is **~$6,000 per student per year**.
>
> HBEF closes about **5% of that operating budget** every year — roughly $1.2–1.5 million flowing directly into instructional programs the state doesn't fund: STEAM, music & band, drama, Spanish, art, librarians, counselors, mental-health support, PE. Without HBEF, those programs disappear from the school day.

Twenty percent of every event dollar flows here, building a permanent base of support for HBCSD that compounds across generations. Appreciated securities welcomed. [endowment@hbef.org]"*

## Numerical conventions

- **Dollars:** USD with `$` prefix. Use `$` not "USD" in headers; "USD" only in cross-border financial contexts.
- **Thousands:** `$1.27M`, `$269K`, `$508,332`. Use K/M abbreviations in tables and headlines; spell out in body prose when precision matters.
- **Percentages:** `5%` not "five percent." Always include sign.
- **Years:** `2025–26 school year` (en-dash, no spaces). `FY 2024–25` for fiscal year references.
- **Dates:** `Saturday, May 9` (informal) or `May 9, 2026` (formal). Avoid `5/9/26` outside spreadsheets.
- **Time:** `6–11pm` (en-dash, lowercase am/pm, no space).
- **Variance:** Show favorable variance with parens for negatives (accounting convention) or with `+`/`-` signs (donor materials). Pick one and stick to it within a single document.
