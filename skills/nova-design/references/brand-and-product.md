# Nova Brand and Product Direction

## Audience

Nova Kids targets children ages 4-8, with the first beta centered on ages 4-5. Many users are pre-readers or early readers. Visual recognition, audio, touch size, and object metaphors matter more than dense text.

## Core Metaphor

Nova is an AI classroom.

- Home is the classroom room view.
- Chalkboard is today's lesson or mission.
- Bookshelf is the lesson library.
- Trophy case is progress and pride.
- Dashy's desk is help, voice, and guidance.
- Workbook is the lesson reader.
- Stickers, tiles, notes, shelves, and boards are interaction materials.

## Differentiator

Nova should not copy classroom education apps. Nova stands out because the classroom changes as parents add generated content and as the child grows. Parent-created lessons become books, board work, missions, stickers, and trophies.

## Age Progression

- Ages 4-5: warm classroom, soft teacher guidance, big objects, simple workbook.
- Ages 6-7: maker lab, tools, projects, experiments, more agency.
- Ages 8+: AI studio, prompt boards, coding desk, creative production.

## Palette

Use these material ideas:

- Chalkboard green for board interiors and active lesson surfaces.
- Warm wood for desks, shelves, frames, and trophy cases.
- Sky blue for windows, calm accents, and listening states.
- Schoolhouse red for new missions, bookmarks, and attention.
- Sunny yellow for rewards, read-aloud buttons, stars, and warmth.
- Paper cream for worksheets, workbook pages, notes, and cards.
- Deep ink navy for outlines and readable text.
- Leaf green for success and growth.

Avoid one-note green/brown classrooms. The room should feel saturated, optimistic, and varied.

## Dashy

Dashy is a classroom guide. Dashy should:

- point to what matters;
- read pages aloud;
- listen during voice prompts;
- celebrate completion;
- help without becoming noisy.

Dashy should not be just an icon in a tab.
