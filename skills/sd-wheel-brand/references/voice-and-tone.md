# SD Wheel — Voice & Tone

## Voice descriptors

Enthusiastic • Confident • Service-forward • Blue-collar aspirational • Informal-expert • Family-warm

## The stance

Write like a knowledgeable friend at a shop counter, not a corporation. The reader is a truck/car enthusiast with fitment anxiety and a budget; SD Wheel removes risk (guaranteed fitment, free mount/balance/ship) and adds swagger ("your ride will never go unnoticed again").

## Real phrases from the wild (use as calibration)

- "Ready To Roll" (hero)
- "Massive Inventory, top-tier service, and easy payment options."
- "Turn your dream build into a reality in just a few clicks."
- "Shipped Free To You — Ready To Install"
- "Enjoy as low as 0% APR Financing - Upgrade Now, Pay Later"
- "Our world-class team of passionate vehicle enthusiasts have decades of experience, knowledge, and drive to help. Just pick up the phone and give us a ring now!"
- "We would never sell you something we wouldn't have on our vehicles."
- "Be a part of the SD Wheel family"

## Lexicon

**Recurring nouns:** dream build, ride, package, fitment, setup, enthusiasts, experts, family, deal.
**Recurring verbs:** roll, build, upgrade, ship, stand out, turn heads, bolt on.
**Vehicle vocabulary:** lifted, leveled, lowered, bagged, offset, backspacing, mud terrain, all terrain.

## Tone by context

- **Hero/social:** high energy, short sentences, imperative ("Start your build at SDWheel.com").
- **Product/education:** plain-spoken expert; explain offset/fitment without condescension; concrete specs.
- **Service/policy:** calm, direct, human — this is where the live site drifts legalistic; keep warmth ("we'll work with you") while staying precise about fees and timelines.
- **B2B/wholesale:** brisker, benefit-bulleted (real-time inventory, flexible payment, exclusive brands), still enthusiast-flavored.

## Do / Don't

**Do:** lead with the package promise (mounted, balanced, free shipping, guaranteed fit); cite real numbers (4.9★ across 3,386 Facebook & Google reviews; since 2003; 140+ brands); invoke the founder garage-to-warehouse story for trust; use "we/you" directly.
**Don't:** use unsubstantiated world-scale superlatives ("#1 in the world," "fastest shipping in the world" — see positioning-and-claims.md); go corporate ("solutions," "leverage synergies"); hedge into mush; overpromise ship dates the ops data can't back.

## Punctuation & mechanics

Exclamation points allowed but max one per paragraph. Contractions always. Second person default. Em-dashes and ampersands fine ("Wheel & Tire Packages"). Emojis only in social captions (🤘 💪 appear in live Shorts captions), never in documents.
