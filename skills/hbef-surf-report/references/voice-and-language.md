# HBEF Surf Report — Voice & Language Reference

The detailed voice rules, phrasings, and patterns specific to writing HBEF articles for the Surf Report. Sibling to `hbef-fb-voice` — same parent voice, different format constraints.

## Sentence rhythm in the Surf Report

A typical HBEF Surf Report article is **3–6 sentences**, each **15–35 words**. The rhythm:

| Sentence | Function | Word target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The whole story in one sentence, with bold key fact | 20–35 |
| 2 | Context: why it matters, who it serves, scale | 15–30 |
| 3 | Specifics: names, numbers, programs, deadlines | 15–25 |
| 4 | Optional supporting beat — partner names, nested Spotlight, list | varies |
| 5 (last) | CTA with inline hyperlink + deadline | 10–20 |

Compare:

**Too long, too wandering (NOT HBEF Surf Report voice):**

> *We at the Hermosa Beach Education Foundation are absolutely thrilled to announce that we have an exciting new initiative this year that we believe will really make a tremendous difference for our students, and we hope you will consider supporting us as we work together to make sure that our schools have the resources they deserve to provide the kind of education that every Hermosa child deserves.*

**On voice:**

> *HBEF is bridging the gap to move our Hermosa View and Vista counselors from **part-time (80%) to 100% full-time status** for the 2026-2027 school year. Our goal is 3 full-time counselors on-site daily to support all 1,450 students!*

Two sentences. Specific schools. Specific percentages. Specific student count. That's the bar.

## Headline patterns

The strongest HBEF Surf Report headlines fit one of five patterns:

| Pattern | Example | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Verb + percentage/goal** | "Help Bring School Counselors to 100%" | Campaign push |
| **Punchy three-beat rhythm** | "Swing Hard. Support Schools. Repeat." | Event promo, themed |
| **Equation framing** | "Summer Plans = Support HBEF" | Sponsor partnership |
| **Event name + Thanks/Feedback** | "Hearts of Hermosa Thanks" | Recap, survey |
| **Direct action verb** | "Vote for HBEF at Lazy Acres" | Vote/action article |

**Headline length: 4–8 words.** Long headlines wrap awkwardly in the Surf Report layout. Short, dense, specific.

**Never:**
- "An Important Update from HBEF" (vague)
- "Please Read: HBEF News" (begging)
- "HBEF News for the Week of May 17" (corporate-newsletter-y)
- "From the HBEF President's Desk" (too formal)

## First-sentence rules

The first sentence is the entire article in compressed form. Every HBEF Surf Report article should pass this test:

> If a reader read ONLY the headline + first sentence, do they know:
> (a) what HBEF is doing, (b) the key date/number, (c) what they should do?

Examples that pass:

> *"Use our exclusive code **26SFHERMOSA** to save **$50 on your Camp Galileo purchase** — Galileo matches the $50 to HBEF."*

> *"There's still time to vote for HBEF — Lazy Acres is selecting partners for their Envirotokens program for next year, and voting closes **June 2nd**."*

> *"HBEF is bridging the gap to move our Hermosa View and Vista counselors from **part-time (80%) to 100% full-time** for the 2026-2027 school year."*

## Bold-for-emphasis policy

Bold is used **frequently** in the Surf Report — far more than in formal documents. It exists to help skim-readers grab key facts.

**Bold these:**
- Dates and deadlines (`May 23–25`, `June 7th`, `Friday, May 22nd`)
- Dollar amounts and percentages (`$50 off`, `100% full-time`, `$1,200`)
- Discount/sale codes (`26SFHERMOSA`)
- School names when first mentioned (`Hermosa View`, `Vista`, `Valley`)
- Time windows (`After school until 3:30PM`)
- Key actions for the reader (`SIGN UP HERE`, `with a parent to qualify`)
- The first occurrence of the main number/goal in an article

**Don't bold:**
- Generic excitement words (don't bold "amazing," "incredible")
- Whole sentences (defeats the purpose)
- More than ~10% of the body text per article

## Vocabulary patterns

### Words HBEF uses regularly in the Surf Report

- *Bridge the gap* (mission shorthand — appears constantly)
- *Funding* (verb and noun) — "funding the programs, teachers, and opportunities…"
- *Community Partner / Community Partners* (capitalized — formal sponsor tier)
- *Support* (verb of choice for parent engagement — "your support makes…")
- *Inspired by* (event recap pivot phrase)
- *We'd love to hear from you* (recruitment close)
- *On-site daily / full-time / part-time* (program staffing precision)
- *Whether you / whether you're* (multi-option recruitment phrasing)
- *Spread the word* (vote/action close)
- *Together, we can…* (collective close)

### Phrases HBEF reuses across articles

| Phrase | Used in | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Help us get to [X]%" | Campaign push | Gamifies the goal, makes it shared |
| "Use our exclusive code…" | Sponsor partnership | Implies insider benefit |
| "We'd love your feedback as we begin planning for next year" | Survey | Forward-looking, parent-as-collaborator |
| "Whether you're interested in [X, Y, or Z]" | Recruitment | Widens the on-ramp |
| "Spread the word to all of your friends, family, and neighbors" | Vote/action | Builds movement-feel |
| "We'd love to hear from you! Email…" | Recruitment close | Inviting, low-pressure |
| "Inspired by [event] and looking for a way to get more involved?" | Recap → recruitment pivot | Bridges archetypes naturally |
| "Don't get left in the bunker" / theme-extended urgency | Event promo | Playful soft-urgency |

### Words to avoid

- "Leverage," "synergy," "stakeholder," "impactful," "robust," "actionable"
- "Reach out" used more than once per article (becomes verbal tic)
- "Excited to announce" (boilerplate corporate-newsletter-y)
- "Without further ado" (filler)
- "At this time" (filler — say "now" or just drop it)
- "Friends of the foundation" (slightly distancing — say "Hermosa families" or "our community")

## Hyperlink etiquette

The Surf Report renders hyperlinks as **underlined blue text** inline in sentences. The link text IS the call-to-action — never use "click here."

**Good link text:**
- "Donate HERE by June 7th"
- "Take the survey here"
- "VOTE for HBEF today"
- "Secure your spot on the green here"
- "Email VicePresident@hbef.org"
- "Sign up for an open shift"

**Bad link text:**
- "click here"
- "this link"
- "more info"
- "read more"

**Link placement:** End of sentence, ideally end of article. One primary link per article. If multiple links, the primary one goes last (Surf Report reads top-to-bottom; readers act on the last thing they saw).

## Bullet lists in articles

The Surf Report renders simple bulleted lists with the `·` character or filled-circle bullets. HBEF articles use lists for:

- **Open volunteer positions** ("Positions include:")
- **Multi-event date listings** ("Field Day events:")
- **Multi-channel CTAs** ("· Take the survey here. · View event photos here.")
- **Shopping hours / event hours**

Keep lists tight — 3–6 items max. If you have more than 6, split into two short articles.

## CTA pattern library

The closing CTA on every HBEF Surf Report article fits one of these patterns:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Donate + deadline** | "Donate HERE by June 7th to help us get to 100%!" |
| **Take + survey link** | "Take the survey here." |
| **Vote + deadline** | "VOTE for HBEF today! Voting closes June 2nd." |
| **Sign-up + spot warning** | "Secure your spot on the green here — spots are filling fast!" |
| **Email + role** | "Email VicePresident@hbef.org to learn more." |
| **Shop + code** | "Use code 26SFHERMOSA at galileo-camps.com." |
| **Share + spread** | "Spread the word to your friends, family, and neighbors!" |

Every article needs one — articles that close with "Thanks for your support!" without a specific next action read as soft, unfinished.

## Tone modifiers by archetype

The HBEF Surf Report voice shifts subtly by article type:

| Archetype | Tone | Example word/phrase |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign Push | Urgent, mission-tied | "bridging the gap," "stronger together" |
| Spotlight | Warm, specific, celebratory | "tremendous impact," "dedication to…" |
| Sponsor Partnership | Insider-friendly, win-win | "exclusive code," "the best part?" |
| Survey / Feedback | Open, collaborative | "we'd love your feedback," "help us…" |
| Recruitment | Inviting, low-pressure | "we'd love to hear from you," "whether you love…" |
| Vote/Action | Civic-rallying | "spread the word," "VOTE for HBEF" |
| Event Promo | Playful, theme-tied | "let's get after it," "rally your crew" |
| Recap/Thanks | Grateful, forward-looking | "thank you to everyone," "looking forward to next year" |

## Things HBEF never does in the Surf Report

- **Repeat the org explainer** every issue. HBEF is named in the section header — readers know who you are.
- **Use third person** ("HBEF announced that…"). Always first-person plural ("We're announcing…" or "HBEF is…").
- **Mix HBEF and HVPTO work in one article.** If something is HVPTO's program, that's HVPTO's article — keep HBEF articles for HBEF work.
- **Use the Facebook emoji palette indiscriminately.** Surf Report is text-forward. Max one emoji per article, and only when it adds meaning (⛳ for golf, ❤️ for Hearts).
- **Hashtag in the body.** No `#HBEF` in article text. The HVPTO Surf Report has `#PoweredByHVPTO` as its global footer, but HBEF articles don't carry their own hashtags.
- **Beg or guilt-trip.** "Please please donate" never. Confident + math.
- **Submit at the last minute.** HVPTO accepts late submissions but they get cut first if the issue is over length.

## Voice comparison cheat sheet

| Surface | Tone | Length | Emoji | Hashtags | CTAs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Facebook** (`hbef-fb-voice`) | Playful, parent-to-parent | 150–600 chars visible | 1–2 from palette | 3–5 at end | UPPERCASE button-style |
| **Surf Report** (this skill) | Newsletter-formal, structured | 80–150 words | 0–1 minimal | None in body | Inline hyperlinks |
| **Email blast** (standalone) | Direct, focused | 100–300 words | 0–1 minimal | None | Inline hyperlinks + signature |
| **Formal doc** (playbook/report) | IC peer / board-formal | 200+ words per section | None | None | Formal recommendations |
| **Press release / news** | Third-person factual | 250+ words | None | None | Contact info at end |
