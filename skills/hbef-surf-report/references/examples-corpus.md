## E1. Campaign Push — Counselors to 100%

Source: Untitled.pdf · ~May 24, 2026 Surf Report

> **Help Bring School Counselors to 100%**
>
> HBEF is bridging the gap to move our Hermosa View and Vista counselors from **part-time (80%) to 100% full-time** status for the 2026-2027 school year. Our goal is **3 full-time counselors on-site daily** to support all 1,450 students!
>
> *Counselor Spotlight: Sabrina Okuda*
>
> Ms. Okuda has made a tremendous impact on the Hermosa View community through her compassion, leadership, and dedication to students' social-emotional growth. From leading engaging PBIS SURF lessons on kindness, leadership, and active listening to organizing meaningful schoolwide experiences like our first Career Day, she helps create a positive, connected culture where every student feels supported and valued.
>
> **Deadline: Donate HERE by June 7th to help us get to 100%!**
>
> Together, we can make Hermosa schools stronger!

**What to study:**
- The headline ("Help Bring School Counselors to **100%**") gamifies the campaign into a single percentage goal.
- Sentence 1 bolds the key transition (part-time 80% → 100% full-time) and the specific schools (View, Vista).
- Sentence 2 quantifies the scale (3 counselors, 1,450 students) in a single concrete claim.
- The nested Spotlight names a specific person (Sabrina Okuda) and her specific contributions (PBIS SURF lessons, Career Day).
- The CTA bolds the deadline (June 7th) and the action (Donate HERE).
- Closes with the collective close: "Together, we can…"

## E4. Volunteer / Board Recruitment

Source: Untitled.pdf · ~May 24, 2026 Surf Report

> **Get Involved with HBEF**
>
> Join the Team for 2026–27!
>
> Do you have a talent for creativity, communications, fundraising, or event planning? The Hermosa Beach Education Foundation (HBEF) is looking for passionate volunteers to join our Board and Committees for the upcoming school year.
>
> Whether you love bringing events to life, building community partnerships, supporting marketing efforts, or helping behind the scenes, your time and talents make a direct impact on every student in Hermosa Schools.
>
> Interested in getting involved? We'd love to hear from you! Email VicePresident@hbef.org to learn more.
>
> Positions include:
> · Marketing Team: Director of Marketing, Social Media Chair, Graphic Designers, Web Designer & Email Newsletter Designer

**What to study:**
- "Join the Team for 2026–27!" — a tagline-ish sub-headline under the article title.
- Opens with a four-option hook question ("Do you have a talent for creativity, communications, fundraising, or event planning?") — multiple on-ramps.
- "Whether you love…" pattern repeats the inclusivity with four more on-ramps.
- "Direct impact on every student" is the impact framing.
- CTA is email (`VicePresident@hbef.org`) — not a form, not a button. Email is more personal.
- "We'd love to hear from you!" is HBEF's signature low-pressure recruitment close.
- Position list at the end is optional but useful — gives concrete role names.

## E5. Community Partner Vote — Envirotokens

Source: Untitled.pdf · ~May 24, 2026 Surf Report

> **Lazy Acres Envirotokens**
>
> There's still time to vote for HBEF! Our generous Community Partner, Lazy Acres, is selecting partners for their Envirotokens program for next year. (If you've shopped at Lazy Acres, you've seen this program in action: Shoppers receive a token for each reusable bag they bring into the store and they can deposit those tokens into the box for the organizations they wish to support.)
>
> Over the last school year, HBEF has earned **over $1,200 — just from Envirotokens!** We need YOUR help to keep HBEF included as an Envirotokens partner for next year: VOTE for HBEF today!
>
> Please spread the word to all of your friends, family, and neighbors, too! Voting closes **June 2nd**.

**What to study:**
- Parenthetical explainer of how Envirotokens works — useful because not every reader knows. Inline parens is more skim-friendly than a separate paragraph.
- Specific dollar earned ($1,200) anchors the article — concrete proof the program works.
- "We need YOUR help" — only emphasized capital here is YOUR (focused on reader agency).
- "VOTE for HBEF today!" — one of the few places HBEF uses all-caps mid-sentence (matches the urgency of a vote close).
- "Please spread the word to all of your friends, family, and neighbors" — three-beat amplification phrase.
- Bolded deadline (`June 2nd`) at the end.

## E8. Surf Report Extra — HVPTO Memorial Day Fundraiser (for format reference)

Source: 2.pdf · May 20, 2026

> **We need your help!**
>
> The HVS Memorial Day Parking Lot Fundraiser is **THIS weekend**, and thanks to our superstar parents and students, Saturday is nearly staffed. But Sunday and Monday? Let's just say those sign-up sheets are looking a little… lonely.
>
> Can you spare just **2 hours** to help fund beloved programs at View, Vista & Valley schools such as field trip transportation, recess equipment, Field Days, assemblies, Outdoor Education, Staff Appreciation Week, Lunch Clubs, Athletics Awards, and many other things that kids think magically appear out of thin air?
>
> Volunteering is simple and surprising fun! You'll collect parking fees and guide cars to available spots in the Valley School parking lots. If you usually can't volunteer because of work, this is your moment to shine!
>
> Middle schoolers earn **8 Service Points** when they volunteer with a parent and younger kiddos are welcome too!
>
> BTW, sorry to be the bearer of bad news, but it's looking like a May Gray weekend. So plan a break from the beach, **SIGN UP** for an open shift, and turn those clouds into sunshine by helping us raise $12,000 for our schools.
>
> Questions: Jeff Ackermann

**What to study (this is HVPTO's, not HBEF's, but the format applies):**
- Surf Report Extras are single-topic, urgent, no-other-content emails.
- Headline is conversational, not formal ("We need your help!").
- Bold for emphasis on every key fact: THIS weekend, 2 hours, 8 Service Points, SIGN UP.
- Specific funded programs are listed inline (six examples) — concrete impact.
- "BTW, sorry to be the bearer of bad news…" is highly conversational — HBEF can match this register when the topic warrants it (e.g., Annual Giving last-call, ticket sellout warning).
- Closes with the dollar goal ($12,000) and a sign-off contact name (Jeff Ackermann).
- HBEF can borrow this format for HBEF-specific Surf Report Extras (urgent ticket-on-sale, AG closer, Hearts last-call).

## Voice diagnostic — "is this HBEF Surf Report?"

When evaluating whether a draft matches HBEF Surf Report voice, check:

| Check | Pass condition |
|---|---|
| Headline | 4–8 words, specific or playful, not vague |
| First sentence | Contains the topic, the key date/number, and the ask — bolded key fact |
| Word count | 80–150 words |
| Specific names | At least one named person, partner, or program |
| Specific numbers | At least one (date, dollar, percentage, deadline) |
| Bold mid-sentence | 2–4 bold phrases for skimmability |
| Hyperlink CTAs | Action-verb link text ("Donate HERE", not "click here") |
| Closing CTA | A clear next step with deadline or contact |
| HBEF vs HVPTO | Article is clearly HBEF's, not HVPTO's |
| Emoji count | 0–1 |
| Hashtags in body | None |
| Image suggestion | Attached or noted in submission |

If a draft fails 3+ checks, rewrite from a formula in `formula-library.md`.
