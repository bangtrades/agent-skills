# HBEF Surf Report — Submission Guide

How HBEF gets its content into the HVS Surf Report, and what to expect from the process.

## The Surf Report ecosystem

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Publication** | HVS Surf Report |
| **Publisher** | HVPTO (Hermosa View, Vista & Valley Parent Teacher Organization) |
| **Cadence** | Weekly — typically Sunday afternoon |
| **Extras** | Mid-week "Surf Report Extra" for urgent single-topic items |
| **Platform** | Constant Contact email service |
| **Subject line format** | "HVPTO Surf Report MM.DD.YY" |
| **Submission inbox** | communications@hvpto.com |
| **Archive** | hvpto.com (every issue posted) |
| **Reach** | All HVS-district families (TK–8) |
| **HBEF's spot** | A dedicated `HBEF` section in the running order, between HVPTO's own articles and the other org sections (HBCSD, Young at Art, etc.) |

## What HBEF controls vs what HVPTO controls

| HBEF controls | HVPTO controls |
|---|---|
| Article headlines | The `HBEF` section divider design |
| Body copy | Article positioning within the issue |
| Image suggestions | Banner, footer, global design |
| Hyperlink destinations | Final layout and image cropping |
| CTAs and deadlines | Editing for length if submission exceeds capacity |
| Number of articles submitted | Whether a submission lands this week or gets bumped |

HBEF does not have authority over the Surf Report's global design, banner, color scheme, or distribution mechanics. The skill is for HBEF's content; the visual chrome belongs to HVPTO.

## Submission deadline & cadence

- **Friday morning** is the safe submission window for the upcoming Sunday issue.
- **Thursday end-of-day** is the better target — gives HVPTO time to lay out, source images, and confirm.
- **Wednesday or earlier** is required for any HBEF content that needs new graphic creation by HVPTO.
- **Surf Report Extras** (mid-week, urgent) — submit at least 24 hours before desired send time, with subject line clearly noting "URGENT: Surf Report Extra — [topic]".

The HBEF owner of Surf Report submissions is **Rob Chong (Surf Report & Room Parent Comms)** per the 2025–26 board roster. Coordinate with Rob first, then he submits to HVPTO; he's also the conduit for image and link coordination with Marketing.

## What to submit

Email `communications@hvpto.com` (CC: Rob Chong if not the sender) with:

1. **Subject:** `HBEF submission for [Issue Date] Surf Report`
2. **Body — for each article you're submitting, include:**
   - **Section** (always `HBEF` for HBEF submissions)
   - **Article headline** (bold)
   - **Article body** (final copy — what you want printed)
   - **Image suggestion** (attached or linked — JPG/PNG, ideally 600px+ wide; HVPTO will resize)
   - **Hyperlinks** (clearly noted in copy: `[link text](URL)`)
   - **Deadline-sensitive** flag if the article needs to ship this week specifically

3. **Multiple articles in one email is fine** — separate clearly with `---` rules.

## What gets accepted, what gets cut

HVPTO almost always accepts well-formed HBEF submissions. Things that get cut or edited:

- **Articles longer than 150 words** — get trimmed (sometimes losing nuance HBEF wanted).
- **Multiple campaigns crammed into one article** — get split, sometimes losing context.
- **Vague headlines** — get rewritten by the editor (often less catchy than HBEF's original).
- **Last-minute submissions** when the issue is already at capacity — get bumped to next week.
- **Articles without clear CTAs** — get edited to add one (often imperfectly).

To avoid edits, write tight, write clear, write punchy. Submit early.

## Image guidance

- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Width:** 600px minimum; HVPTO resizes
- **Aspect:** Square or 4:3 portrait works best in the right-column layout
- **Content:** Event photos (credit Jennifer Faulk Photography where applicable), program photos (with student permissions if minors are recognizable), partner/sponsor logos, themed graphics
- **Naming:** Include topic in filename — `counselor-spotlight-okuda.jpg`, `strand-classic-promo.jpg`
- **One image per article max** — HVPTO lays out images right-of-text in a single-column slot per article

## Frequency norms — how often HBEF submits

Per the FY history seen in 2025–26 issues:

| Time of year | HBEF article count per issue |
|---|---|
| Aug–Sep (Annual Giving open) | 3–5 articles |
| Oct–Nov (Walk + fall stewardship) | 2–4 articles |
| Dec–Jan (Holiday/Giving Tuesday) | 2–3 articles |
| Feb–Mar (MNO + Hearts ramp-up) | 3–5 articles |
| Apr–May (Hearts + wrap) | 2–4 articles |
| Jun–Jul (school out, summer slow) | 0–2 articles |

If a week's HBEF content is light, submit 1 evergreen volunteer/board-recruitment article — it's the easiest reliable filler and keeps HBEF's section visible.

## Cross-channel coordination

Surf Report submissions should be coordinated with:

- **Facebook + Instagram (`hbef-fb-voice`)** — match talking points but rewrite for each surface (FB voice is shorter and more playful)
- **HBEF website (hbef.org/events, hbef.org/news)** — link destinations should be live before the Surf Report ships
- **Marketing Team** — for graphic creation and visual alignment
- **Co-Directors of Sponsorship** — for any article touching a Community Partner relationship (give them a heads-up before submission)
- **HBEF President (Belinda Oakes)** — for campaign-level or impact-claim articles (factcheck dollar amounts before submission)

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Submitting last-minute without coordinating with Rob Chong** — bypassing the established HBEF/HVPTO workflow creates friction
- **Submitting articles that conflict with HVPTO programming** — if HVPTO already has a parking-lot fundraiser article in the same issue, HBEF probably shouldn't pile on with three more fundraising articles. Coordinate.
- **Treating the Surf Report as press release distribution** — it's a community newsletter. Voice matters more than information density.
- **Submitting articles older than this week's news** — if HBEF announced X last week on Facebook, the Surf Report version needs a fresh angle ("Update on X" / "Final week for X") or skip it.
- **Forgetting the image** — the layout breaks visually without an image; the article reads as a wall of text
- **Sending the article without the hyperlinks live** — embarrassing when a parent clicks and lands on a 404

## After submission

After the Sunday issue ships:

1. Confirm the HBEF section ran as expected (link, image, headline).
2. Note any edits HVPTO made — useful intel for future submissions.
3. Track engagement (donations, sign-ups, votes) attributable to the Surf Report.
4. Capture lessons learned in the running submission log so the team improves issue over issue.
