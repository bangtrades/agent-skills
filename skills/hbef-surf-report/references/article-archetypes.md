# HBEF Surf Report Article Archetypes

The eight recurring HBEF article types in the Surf Report. Each has a structural template and a real-example excerpt from prior issues. For fill-in-the-blank speed, see `formula-library.md`.

---

## 1. Campaign / Program Push

**When:** Active fundraising for a specific staff line or program (counselor coverage, librarian, lab build).

**Goal:** Drive donations against a quantified goal with a specific deadline.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** the % or program name. "Help Bring School Counselors to 100%" / "Fund a Full-Time Librarian for View."
2. **Sentence 1:** What HBEF is doing and the specific target. Bold the key fact (the percentage, the dollar, the campus).
3. **Sentence 2–3:** Why it matters. Concrete student impact, names of schools, scale (X students, Y campuses).
4. **Optional nested Spotlight feature** (see archetype #2) — a person, program, or moment that exemplifies the campaign.
5. **Closing CTA:** "Donate HERE by [deadline]!" — inline hyperlink with the action verb.

**Real example (Untitled.pdf · ~May 24, 2026):**

> **Help Bring School Counselors to 100%**
>
> HBEF is bridging the gap to move our Hermosa View and Vista counselors from **part-time (80%) to 100% full-time status** for the 2026-2027 school year. Our goal is 3 full-time counselors on-site daily to support all 1,450 students!
>
> *Counselor Spotlight: Sabrina Okuda*
>
> Ms. Okuda has made a tremendous impact on the Hermosa View community through her compassion, leadership, and dedication to students' social-emotional growth…
>
> **Deadline: Donate HERE by June 7th to help us get to 100%!**
>
> Together, we can make Hermosa schools stronger!

## 2. Spotlight Feature

**When:** Whenever a counselor, teacher, librarian, sponsor, or board member deserves named recognition. Usually nested *inside* another article (campaign push, partnership promo, recap).

**Goal:** Personalize the impact. Make the abstract concrete by naming the human.

**Structure:**
1. **Sub-headline:** "Counselor Spotlight: [Name]" / "Sponsor Spotlight: [Company]" / "Teacher Spotlight: [Name]" — italic or bold.
2. **Sentence 1:** What this person/entity has done — specific contribution, role, tenure.
3. **Sentence 2:** Specific examples of impact — a program they ran, a kid they helped, a number they hit.
4. **(No standalone CTA — the parent article carries it.)**

**Real example (nested inside Counselors campaign):**

> *Counselor Spotlight: Sabrina Okuda*
>
> Ms. Okuda has made a tremendous impact on the Hermosa View community through her compassion, leadership, and dedication to students' social-emotional growth. From leading engaging PBIS SURF lessons on kindness, leadership, and active listening to organizing meaningful schoolwide experiences like our first Career Day, she helps create a positive, connected culture where every student feels supported and valued.

**Note:** Spotlights work best when the named person has clearly given permission and when the surrounding article justifies why they're highlighted in context (campaign tie, anniversary, retirement). Don't randomly spotlight someone without a hook.

## 3. Sponsor Partnership Promo

**When:** A Community Partner is offering Hermosa families a discount, code, or match that benefits HBEF.

**Goal:** Drive purchases through the partner's offer while making the HBEF benefit clear.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** clever or direct — "Summer Plans = Support HBEF" / "Shop Local, Fund HBEF."
2. **Sentence 1:** Specific offer with code or link. Bold the dollar savings or code.
3. **Sentence 2:** The match-back-to-HBEF mechanic — exactly how HBEF benefits.
4. **CTA:** Where to redeem (URL, code at checkout, in-store mention).

**Real example (Untitled.pdf · Camp Galileo):**

> **Summer Plans = Support HBEF**
>
> Finalizing your summer schedule? Use our exclusive code **26SFHERMOSA** to save **$50 on your Camp Galileo purchase**. The best part? Galileo will match that $50 as a donation to HBEF!
>
> Go to galileo-camps.com to browse the 2026 projects!

**Note:** Codes go in **ALL CAPS** when they're literal redemption codes — this is the exception to the "no all-caps" rule. The reader needs to recognize them as something to type.

## 4. Survey / Feedback Request

**When:** Post-event (Hearts of Hermosa, MNO, Skechers Walk) or pre-planning for next year.

**Goal:** Get parent input + show HBEF listens.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** "[Event] Feedback" / "Help HBEF Make Next Year's [Event] Even Better."
2. **Sentence 1:** Frame why feedback matters — for next year's planning.
3. **Sentence 2:** Reassure: short survey, anonymous, all welcome (attendees + non-attendees both).
4. **CTA:** Survey link inline. "Take the survey here."

**Real example (Untitled.pdf):**

> **HoH Feedback**
>
> Help HBEF make next year's Hearts of Hermosa even better… Whether you attended or not, please share your thoughts in this quick survey — your feedback is most appreciated!
>
> Hearts of Hermosa 2026 Survey

**Note:** Surveys work best when the article explicitly invites non-attendees. The most useful feedback often comes from people who *didn't* go and can say why.

## 5. Volunteer / Board Recruitment

**When:** Spring/summer board cycle. June–July submissions hit hardest because parents are thinking about next school year.

**Goal:** Convert curious parents into Committee Members or specific role-fillers.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** "Get Involved with HBEF" / "Join the HBEF Team."
2. **Sentence 1:** Direct invitation — "We're looking for…" or "Join the Team for [year]!"
3. **Sentence 2:** What kinds of skills/interests fit. Cast wide: creativity, communications, fundraising, event planning.
4. **Sentence 3:** "Whether you love X, Y, or Z…" — multiple on-ramps.
5. **CTA:** Email contact (typically `VicePresident@hbef.org` for general; specific Chair emails for specific roles).
6. **Optional list of open positions** — under "Positions include:" header. 3–5 named roles max.

**Real example (Untitled.pdf):**

> **Get Involved with HBEF**
>
> Join the Team for 2026–27!
>
> Do you have a talent for creativity, communications, fundraising, or event planning? The Hermosa Beach Education Foundation (HBEF) is looking for passionate volunteers to join our Board and Committees for the upcoming school year.
>
> Whether you love bringing events to life, building community partnerships, supporting marketing efforts, or helping behind the scenes, your time and talents make a direct impact on every student in Hermosa Schools.
>
> Interested in getting involved? We'd love to hear from you! Email VicePresident@hbef.org to learn more.
>
> Positions include:
> · Marketing Team: Director of Marketing, Social Media Chair, Graphic Designers, Web Designer & Email Newsletter Designer

**Note:** Recruitment articles can run long (toward 150 words) because they need to give parents a few "in" points. Lists work well here.

## 6. Community Partner Vote

**When:** Lazy Acres Envirotokens cycle (annual), Box Tops, similar.

**Goal:** Drive votes/clicks to keep HBEF eligible for the partner's giving program.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** Specific partner + action. "Lazy Acres Envirotokens" / "Vote for HBEF at Lazy Acres."
2. **Sentence 1:** What's happening + why HBEF is at stake.
3. **Sentence 2–3:** Brief explainer of how the program works (in parens if it can be tightened).
4. **Specific impact:** What HBEF has earned through it — "$1,200 last year just from Envirotokens."
5. **CTA:** "VOTE for HBEF today!" + URL + deadline.
6. **Close:** "Please spread the word…"

**Real example (Untitled.pdf):**

> **Lazy Acres Envirotokens**
>
> There's still time to vote for HBEF! Our generous Community Partner, Lazy Acres, is selecting partners for their Envirotokens program for next year. (If you've shopped at Lazy Acres, you've seen this program in action: Shoppers receive a token for each reusable bag they bring into the store and they can deposit those tokens into the box for the organizations they wish to support.)
>
> Over the last school year, HBEF has earned **over $1,200 — just from Envirotokens!** We need YOUR help to keep HBEF included as an Envirotokens partner for next year: VOTE for HBEF today!
>
> Please spread the word to all of your friends, family, and neighbors, too! Voting closes June 2nd.

**Note:** This archetype is one of the rare cases where ALL CAPS is acceptable — for `VOTE for HBEF` because it's a literal verb-action and matches the urgency of a vote close.

## 7. Event Promo

**When:** 4–8 weeks before any HBEF event — Hearts of Hermosa, Skechers Walk, MNO, Santa Photos, Strand Classic, Flash Parties.

**Goal:** Drive registration / ticket purchase before sellout.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** Theme-tied or punchy. "Swing Hard. Support Schools. Repeat." / "Hearts of Hermosa Is Almost Here."
2. **Sentence 1:** What it is, when, and one reason to come. Bold the date.
3. **Sentence 2–3:** Format, partners, vibe — concrete details, theme extension if applicable.
4. **Soft urgency:** "Spots are filling fast" / "Sells out every year."
5. **CTA:** "Secure your spot here!" — inline hyperlink.

**Real example (Untitled.pdf · The Strand Classic):**

> **Swing Hard. Support Schools. Repeat.**
>
> ⛳ Do you like golf? We've already had an awesome turnout for team signups for the inaugural HBEF Strand Classic, and spots are filling fast.
>
> This is a 3-person scramble with both a competitive tier and a social tier. So if you're good at golf, let's get after it. And if you're not good at golf… let's get after it too.
>
> Two teams play together, so rally your crew and get your friends to sign up alongside you for the full experience.
>
> We'll have amazing sponsors plus food and beverage activations across the course all day long. Don't get left in the bunker — grab your threesome and sign up before spots are gone!
>
> Secure your spot on the green here!

**Note:** Event promos can run slightly longer (toward 150 words) because they need to sell the format. They're also where HBEF's signature playful theme extension shows up most clearly — "Don't get left in the bunker" is golf-themed humor that fits the Strand Classic perfectly.

## 8. Event Recap / Thanks

**When:** Within 7 days of an HBEF event closing.

**Goal:** Celebrate the result, thank the community, drive forward to the next thing.

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** "[Event] Thanks" or punchier — "We Did It, Hermosa!" / "Hearts of Hermosa: $X Raised."
2. **Sentence 1:** Thank everyone who showed up — name the event explicitly.
3. **Sentence 2:** Impact framing — "Because of this incredible community, HBEF can continue funding…"
4. **Sentence 3:** Survey link OR photo gallery link OR both. Always invite continued engagement.
5. **Optional pivot:** "Inspired and want to get more involved? HBEF is recruiting…" — soft pivot to the recruitment archetype.
6. **CTA:** Survey + photos + contact email.

**Real example (3.pdf · Hearts of Hermosa 2026 Recap):**

> **Hearts of Hermosa Thanks**
>
> Thank you to everyone who attended, supported, volunteered, donated, bid, danced, and celebrated with us at this year's Hearts of Hermosa! Because of this incredible community, HBEF can continue funding the programs, teachers, and opportunities that make Hermosa schools so special.
>
> We'd love your feedback as we begin planning for next year. Please take a few minutes to complete our quick anonymous survey and help us continue creating meaningful events that bring our community together while supporting our schools.
>
> · Take the survey here.
> · View event photos here.
>
> Inspired by Hearts of Hermosa and looking for a way to get more involved? HBEF is currently recruiting Board Members and Volunteer Committee Members for the 2026–2027 school year. Whether you're interested in annual giving, graphic design, marketing & communications, events, sponsorships, auction support, or helping behind the scenes, there's a place for everyone to make an impact.
>
> As a 100% volunteer-run nonprofit, every role directly supports Hermosa schools and students.
>
> Interested in learning more? Reach out to VicePresident@hbef.org — we'd love to connect!

**Note:** This recap is on the longer end because it bundles recap + recruitment. Most pure recaps stay tighter — 80–120 words — with just the thanks, survey, and a one-sentence next-event tease.

## Composite articles

Several real Surf Report HBEF articles blend two archetypes:

- **Recap + Recruitment** (the canonical Hearts of Hermosa thanks)
- **Campaign Push + Spotlight** (counselor campaign with embedded Ms. Okuda spotlight)
- **Event Promo + Sponsor Spotlight** (Strand Classic mention with food/beverage partners named)
- **Survey + Forward-Looking** (HoH feedback with "help us plan next year" framing)

When blending, follow the dominant archetype's structure and slot the secondary beat in just before the CTA. Don't try to do all four beats of two archetypes — the article will exceed 150 words and get cut.

---

## What NOT to include in a Surf Report article

- **Org-explainer sentences.** Readers already know what HBEF is — don't waste 30 words explaining the nonprofit. Save that for first-time-reader contexts (donor pitches, prospect emails).
- **Tax-deduction language.** It's well-known. Only include if the article explicitly asks for a direct donation.
- **Multiple campaigns in one article.** One topic per piece. If you have five things to share, write five articles.
- **Hashtags.** Surf Report doesn't use them in HBEF article bodies.
- **Emoji clusters.** Surf Report is text-forward; one tasteful emoji (⛳, ❤️, 📚) max.
- **All-caps mid-sentence** except for codes (`26SFHERMOSA`) and verb-actions for vote-type articles (`VOTE for HBEF`).
- **"Click here" CTAs.** The link text IS the action — "Donate HERE," "Take the survey here," "Secure your spot here."
