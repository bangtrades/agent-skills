# HBEF Surf Report — Skill

This skill captures the voice, structure, and design conventions of the **HVS Surf Report** — the weekly community newsletter published by HVPTO (Hermosa View, Vista & Valley Parent Teacher Organization) — as they apply to **HBEF's contributions**. The Surf Report is the most reliably-read communication channel reaching every Hermosa family, and HBEF has its own dedicated section in every issue.

The skill covers two related use cases:

1. **The primary use case:** Drafting HBEF's content that gets submitted to `communications@hvpto.com` for inclusion in the weekly Surf Report. HBEF does not control the banner, layout, or distribution of the Surf Report — that's HVPTO's. HBEF *does* control the words, the article titles, the CTAs, and the impact stories that appear inside the HBEF section.

2. **The secondary use case:** Building a standalone HBEF email blast that mirrors the Surf Report look-and-feel — useful for HBEF-only urgent announcements (Hearts of Hermosa save-the-dates, Annual Giving openers, ticket-on-sale alarms) that don't fit the Surf Report cycle.

## Voice DNA (one paragraph)

The HBEF Surf Report voice is **newsletter-formal**, slightly more structured than the Facebook voice but with the same warm parent-led core. Each HBEF article in the Surf Report is **a self-contained 80–150 word unit** with a punchy headline, a bolded key fact in the first sentence, a specific impact story, and a clear inline-link CTA. It rewards skim-readers (every article should make sense from the headline + first sentence alone) while keeping the parent-to-parent register the wider community knows. **Headlines can be playful** ("Swing Hard. Support Schools. Repeat.") but should never be vague. Numbers stay specific ($1,200 from Envirotokens, 100% counseling coverage, 1,450 students). Every article ends with a clear next action: a click-through link, a deadline, or an email contact. **If a draft sounds like it could appear in a corporate fundraising appeal, it's wrong.** It should sound like a smart parent quickly catching the community up on what HBEF is doing this week.

## Core voice rules (the short version)

1. **Self-contained articles** — every piece must make sense on its own. Surf Report readers skim; some only read your section.
2. **Punchy headline, specific opener** — "Help Bring School Counselors to 100%" not "An Important Update from HBEF."
3. **Bold the key fact in sentence one** — the date, the dollar, the deadline, or the program name.
4. **Specific numbers** — `$1,200`, `1,450 students`, `100%`, `$50 off`. Never vague ("a lot," "many," "significant").
5. **Inline hyperlink CTAs** — `Donate HERE by June 7th to help us get to 100%!` or `Take the survey here.` Hyperlinks are the action.
6. **Name names** — sponsors (Lazy Acres, Camp Galileo), people (Sabrina Okuda, Belinda Oakes), specific schools (View, Vista, Valley).
7. **Length cap: 80–150 words per article** — HVPTO will cut anything longer. The shorter, the better.
8. **End with a next action** — deadline + link, contact email, or "spread the word" close.
9. **HBEF vs HVPTO clarity** — never let a reader confuse who funds what. HBEF = programs/staff; HVPTO = supplies/field trips.
10. **Bold for mid-sentence emphasis** — important dates, key terms, deadlines.
11. **Submit by HVPTO's deadline** — typically Friday morning for the following Sunday issue. (See `submission-guide.md`.)

| # | Archetype | When | Signature beats |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Campaign / Program Push** | Active fundraising window (counselors, librarians, lab build) | "Help us get to X%" framing + deadline + donate link |
| 2 | **Spotlight Feature** | Whenever a person should be celebrated (counselor, teacher, sponsor) | Nested sub-headline + 2–3 sentences of personal impact |
| 3 | **Sponsor Partnership Promo** | A Community Partner discount or matching offer is active | Code or link + dollar savings + match-to-HBEF mechanic |
| 4 | **Survey / Feedback Request** | After an event, or planning the next one | "Help HBEF make next year's X even better" + survey link |

| 7 | **Event Promo** | 4–8 weeks before any HBEF event | Date + format + ticket/registration link + "spots filling fast" |
| 8 | **Event Recap / Thanks** | Within 7 days of an HBEF event closing | "Thanks to everyone who…" + impact + survey link + next-event tease |

## Surf Report-specific writing rules

Different from the Facebook voice in five specific ways:

### 1. Headline first, hook in sentence one
On Facebook, the post is the unit. In the Surf Report, the **bold-blue headline** is the unit — it has to earn the read. Headlines should be **5–8 words**, ideally specific or playful.

- ✅ "Help Bring School Counselors to 100%"
- ✅ "Swing Hard. Support Schools. Repeat."
- ✅ "Summer Plans = Support HBEF"
- ✅ "Hearts of Hermosa Thanks"
- ❌ "Important HBEF Update" (vague)
- ❌ "Please Read: News from the Education Foundation" (begging)

### 2. The first sentence carries the whole article
Surf Report readers skim. Sentence one must contain: **the topic, the key date or number, and (often) the ask**. Bold the key fact within it.

> *"HBEF is bridging the gap to move our Hermosa View and Vista counselors from **part-time (80%) to 100% full-time status** for the 2026-2027 school year."*

You could read just that sentence and the headline and know exactly what's happening. That's the bar.

### 3. Inline CTAs, not standalone buttons
The Surf Report renders inline-hyperlinks as the action — no buttons. The hyperlink text is the verb:

- ✅ "Donate HERE by June 7th to help us get to 100%!"
- ✅ "Take the survey here."
- ✅ "Secure your spot on the green here!"
- ✅ "VOTE for HBEF today!"
- ❌ "Click the button below."
- ❌ "Visit hbef.org for more info."

### 4. Articles are 80–150 words. Hard cap.
HVPTO trims long submissions. A clean 100-word article ships intact; a 250-word article gets edited (or cut). Plan accordingly.

### 5. Multiple short articles beats one long one
If HBEF has a lot to say in one issue, split into multiple short articles each with its own headline. The May 24 issue had **five** HBEF articles (Counselors, Camp Galileo, HoH Survey, Board Recruitment, Envirotokens) — that's better than one mega-article covering all five topics.

## Voice carry-over from hbef-fb-voice

Most HBEF voice rules carry across from Facebook. Keep these:

- First-person plural ("we", "our schools", "our Hermosa community")
- Specific dollar amounts (`$1,265,000`, `$269,812`, `$1,200`)
- Names of partners, sponsors, board members
- "Funding matters. Your donation, their future." as occasional close
- HBEF vs HVPTO clarity
- No corporate jargon, no begging, no guilt-tripping

**What changes for the Surf Report:**
- Drop the Facebook-style emoji palette. The Surf Report uses **almost no emoji** (occasional ⛳ for golf, ❤️ for Hearts, but very restrained).
- Use **bold mid-sentence** instead of UPPERCASE CTAs — Surf Report convention is sentence-case headlines and bold-for-emphasis, not all-caps.
- Hashtags are not used in the Surf Report article body.
- Tax language is not required in every article — HBEF's nonprofit status is well-known to Surf Report readers.

## Section header convention

Every HBEF block in the Surf Report opens with the centered red `HBEF` section divider. You don't write this — HVPTO inserts it. But know it's there: it gives every HBEF article the "HBEF section" visual context, so your headline can dive straight into the topic without re-introducing HBEF.

## Submission workflow

For Surf Report submissions, follow this short workflow (full detail in `references/submission-guide.md`):

1. Identify the archetype (see table above).
2. Draft the article using the matching formula from `references/formula-library.md`.
3. Cap at 80–150 words. Cut ruthlessly.
4. Include image suggestion if relevant (HVPTO often uses an image to the right of the article — they pull from HBEF's image library or you can attach one).
5. Submit by **Friday morning** for that Sunday's issue: email body or Google Doc to `communications@hvpto.com`. Lead with "HBEF submission for the [date] Surf Report."
6. Confirm receipt — turnaround is fast but not always automatic.
7. Coordinate with HBEF Co-Director of Marketing on visual + image (when one ships).

## Quick recipe — "I need an HBEF Surf Report blurb on X"

1. **Identify the archetype** from the table above.
2. **Read the matching template** in `references/article-archetypes.md`.
3. **Pull a formula** from `references/formula-library.md`.
4. **Draft sentence one** to carry the whole story with a bolded key fact.
5. **Add 2–4 supporting sentences** with specific names/numbers/deadlines.
6. **Close with the CTA** — inline hyperlink + deadline.
7. **Trim to 80–150 words.** Cut every word that doesn't add information.
8. **Verify against `examples-corpus.md`** to confirm tone.
9. **Submit by Friday morning** to `communications@hvpto.com`.

## Final guardrails

- **Read the corpus before improvising.** `references/examples-corpus.md` has real HBEF Surf Report articles with dates. The voice is in those words.
- **Lengths:** 80–150 words per article. Below 80 is fine for a quick tease; above 150 risks getting cut.
- **Subject lines** (for standalone HBEF emails only): "HBEF: [topic]" or specific event name. Surf Report subjects are HVPTO-controlled ("HVPTO Surf Report MM.DD.YY").
- **Every HBEF Surf Report article should have an image suggestion** — HVPTO almost always lays out HBEF articles with an image to the right. Image specs in `design-system.md`.
- **When in doubt**, ask: would a busy Hermosa parent skim this article in 8 seconds and know exactly what HBEF wants them to do? If yes, ship it. If no, rewrite the headline and the first sentence.
