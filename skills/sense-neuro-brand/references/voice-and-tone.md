# Sense Neuro — Voice & Tone

## Voice in five words
Clinical · urgent · evidence-led · mission-driven · regulator-aware.

## The core idea
**"Time is brain."** Every minute between injury and correct treatment costs irreversible brain function. Sense exists to compress that time with objective, real-time information where none exists today — in the field, in the ambulance, between scans, on the battlefield.

## Tone rules
- **Lead with the unmet need, then the solution.** The site's strongest move: state the gap ("There is no accurate, objective way to detect stroke subtype or brain hemorrhage in the field…") *before* the product.
- **Quantify, don't adjective.** "Single scan in 2.5 seconds," "360 data points," "under four minutes," "detected a 3 mL bleed five hours before standard of care" — numbers carry the credibility. Avoid "revolutionary/cutting-edge" inflation.
- **Speak to clinicians and investors as peers.** Use correct terminology (ICH, LVO, non-LVO, neurocritical care, triage, point of care) without over-explaining.
- **Stay humble about stage.** "In development," "designed to," "intended to." Never imply clearance or established efficacy.
- **Mission over hype.** Warmth comes from the patient stakes (disability, families, first responders), not from superlatives.

## Lexicon
**Use:** stroke, traumatic brain injury (TBI), intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH), large vessel occlusion (LVO), non-LVO, triage, point of care, real-time, non-invasive, the field, cranial vault, first responders, neurocritical care, detect / monitor / triage / differentiate.

**Avoid:** "cure," "guarantees," "diagnoses" (use "designed to detect/assess"), "revolutionary," "game-changing" (the UC piece uses it editorially — keep it out of Sense's own claims), patient-marketing softness.

## Real voice samples (lift the cadence, not verbatim)
- Hero: *"When treating stroke and brain injury, time to proper treatment is critical. Time is brain."*
- Need: *"There is no accurate, objective way to detect stroke subtype or brain hemorrhage in a field environment or to monitor ongoing brain injury continuously, non-invasively and in real time."*
- Solution: *"Non-invasive brain scanners that will assess and monitor the neurological status of a patient in real time."*
- Founder (Adeoye): *"The ability to detect brain bleeds in the field, within minutes, represents a paradigm shift in emergency neurocritical care."*
- CEO (Kurdziel): *"By empowering first responders and clinicians with real-time brain data, NeuroHawk has the potential to transform neurocritical care…"*

## Channel notes
- **LinkedIn** (primary): milestone announcements, founder/SAB thought leadership, DoD/clinical wins. Professional, no emoji-spam.
- **Press releases:** dateline city + bolded news, a CEO quote and a clinical (CMO/SAB) quote, the investigational-device disclaimer at the foot.
- **Investor materials:** lead with the catalyst (FDA submission), the dual-use TAM, razor/blade economics, and the credentialed bench.
- **Defense materials:** factual, capability-and-readiness framing ("under four minutes, point of injury, field-rugged").

## Boilerplate (reusable "About Sense")
*Sense Neuro Diagnostics is a clinical-stage neurodiagnostics company developing non-invasive, radio-frequency–based brain scanners to rapidly detect and triage stroke and traumatic brain injury — and continuously monitor brain bleeds — at the point of care, from the ambulance and emergency department to the ICU and the battlefield. Sense's technology is investigational and not yet cleared or approved by the U.S. FDA.*
