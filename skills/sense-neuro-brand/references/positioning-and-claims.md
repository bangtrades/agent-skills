# Sense Neuro — Positioning & Claims Discipline

## Positioning statement
For emergency clinicians, hospital systems, and defense-medicine buyers who need objective brain assessment where imaging isn't available or fast enough, **Sense Neuro's NeuroHawk™ platform** is a non-invasive RF brain scanner that detects and triages stroke subtype and TBI — and monitors bleeds continuously — in minutes at the point of care. Unlike single-purpose prehospital triage devices (Forest Devices, MindRhythm) or heavy/slow imaging (portable MRI), Sense addresses the **full acute chain in one platform** and is **dual-use** (civilian + DoD-funded military).

## The wedge (how to differentiate)
- **vs Forest Devices (AlphaStroke, EEG):** they triage LVO only and are already FDA-cleared — concede the clearance lead, win on **breadth** (ICH + LVO + non-LVO + TBI + monitoring) and **dual-use**.
- **vs MindRhythm (Harmony, HeadPulse):** single-purpose LVO ID — same breadth wedge.
- **vs Hyperfine (Swoop portable MRI):** real imaging but capital-heavy and ~30 min — win on **speed, portability, field/point-of-injury** use.
- **vs Brainomix (AI on CT/CTA):** software that needs a scan to already exist — win on **pre-scan / pre-hospital** coverage.
- **Pre-empt the NovaSignal cautionary tale:** answer "how does neuro-triage make money?" with **razor/blade economics + DoD procurement** (reimbursement-independent).

## CLAIMS DISCIPLINE — mandatory for anything external

This is a **pre-clearance medical device.** FTC + FDA exposure is real. Every external deliverable must:

1. **Investigational disclaimer (verbatim-equivalent):** "NeuroHawk™ [and/or NeuSTAT™] is an investigational device and is not yet cleared or approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). It is not available for sale or clinical use in the United States."
2. **Data framing:** all human data is **feasibility / early-stage**. Add: "The studies summarized are feasibility or early-stage evaluations. Results have not been reviewed by the U.S. FDA and do not imply device clearance or approval."
3. **Performance hedging:** "Performance characteristics described are based on preliminary, non-clinical, or early-stage clinical evaluations and may not reflect final device performance following FDA review."
4. **Verb discipline:** "designed to," "intended to," "in development," "has the potential to." Never "diagnoses," "proven," "guarantees," "FDA-approved."
5. **Forward-looking statements** (investor docs): "Certain statements are forward-looking and subject to risks and uncertainties. Actual results may differ materially depending on regulatory review, funding, and other factors."
6. **Trademarks:** ™ on first mention of NeuroHawk and NeuSTAT.
7. **Current status only:** FDA = **De Novo submitted Sept 2025, under review.** Never imply a clearance date.
